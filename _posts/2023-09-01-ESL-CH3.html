1. (Ex 3.6) Show that the ridge regression estimate is the mean (and mode) of the posterior distribution, under a Gaussian prior β∼Np(0,τ2Ip)\beta \sim \mathcal N_p(0, \tau^2 I_p)β∼Np​(0,τ2Ip​) and Gaussian sampling model y∼NN(Xβ,σ2IN)y \sim \mathcal N_N(X\beta, \sigma^2 I_N)y∼NN​(Xβ,σ2IN​) with λ=σ2/τ2\lambda = \sigma^2/\tau^2λ=σ2/τ2.

Solution

The posterior distribution of β\betaβ is p(β∣y)∝p(y∣β)p(β)p(\beta | y) \propto p(y | \beta) p(\beta)p(β∣y)∝p(y∣β)p(β).
p(y∣β)∝exp⁡{−12σ2∣y−Xβ∣2}p(y | \beta) \propto \exp \left\{ -\frac{1}{2\sigma^2} | y - X\beta |^2 \right\}p(y∣β)∝exp{−2σ21​∣y−Xβ∣2} and
p(β)∝exp⁡{−τ22∣β∣2}p(\beta) \propto \exp \left\{ -\frac{\tau^2}{2} |\beta|^2 \right\}p(β)∝exp{−2τ2​∣β∣2}.

Thus,
p(y∣β)p(β)∝exp⁡{−12(1σ2∣y−Xβ∣2+τ2∣β∣2)}p(y | \beta) p(\beta) \propto \exp \left\{ -\frac{1}{2} \left( \frac{1}{\sigma^2} | y - X\beta |^2 + \tau^2 |\beta|^2 \right) \right\}p(y∣β)p(β)∝exp{−21​(σ21​∣y−Xβ∣2+τ2∣β∣2)}.

This can be rewritten as:
∝exp⁡{−12(β−μβ)′Σ−1(β−μβ)}\propto \exp \left\{ -\frac{1}{2} (\beta - \mu_\beta)' \Sigma^{-1} (\beta - \mu_\beta) \right\}∝exp{−21​(β−μβ​)′Σ−1(β−μβ​)}.

The quadratic form in the exponential should be identical, so
1σ2(y−Xβ)′(y−Xβ)+τ22β′β=(β−μβ)′Σ−1(β−μβ)∀β\frac{1}{\sigma^2} (y - X\beta)' (y - X\beta) + \frac{\tau^2}{2} \beta' \beta = (\beta - \mu_\beta)' \Sigma^{-1} (\beta - \mu_\beta) \quad \forall \betaσ21​(y−Xβ)′(y−Xβ)+2τ2​β′β=(β−μβ​)′Σ−1(β−μβ​)∀β.

Expanding,
1σ2β′X′Xβ+τ22β′β=β′Σ−1β\frac{1}{\sigma^2} \beta' X' X \beta + \frac{\tau^2}{2} \beta' \beta = \beta' \Sigma^{-1} \betaσ21​β′X′Xβ+2τ2​β′β=β′Σ−1β.

Thus,
Σ−1=1σ2X′X+τ22Ip=1σ2(X′X+τ22σ2Ip)\Sigma^{-1} = \frac{1}{\sigma^2} X' X + \frac{\tau^2}{2} I_p = \frac{1}{\sigma^2} (X'X + \frac{\tau^2}{2} \sigma^2 I_p)Σ−1=σ21​X′X+2τ2​Ip​=σ21​(X′X+2τ2​σ2Ip​).

For the mean,
−1σ2β′X′y=−β′Σ−1μβ-\frac{1}{\sigma^2} \beta' X' y = - \beta' \Sigma^{-1} \mu_\beta−σ21​β′X′y=−β′Σ−1μβ​,
